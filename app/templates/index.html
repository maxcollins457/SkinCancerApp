Cancer Classification App

This app helps classify skin lesions for signs of cancer. The model is trained on a dataset of pigmented skin lesions categorized into different types. The goal is to assist in identifying potentially dangerous skin lesions through machine learning.

Data Source: MNIST HAM10000 Dataset on Kaggle

Types of Skin Lesions

The project uses a dataset of pigmented skin lesions, which are categorized into seven different types. Here is a brief list of each category:

  • Melanocytic nevi
  • Melanoma
  • Benign keratosis-like lesions
  • Basal cell carcinoma
  • Actinic keratoses
  • Vascular lesions
  • Dermatofibroma

Melanocytic nevi

Melanocytic nevi are typically benign moles or nevi, which are non-cancerous skin lesions. They are common and usually harmless.

More Info

Melanoma

Melanoma is a malignant skin cancer that develops from melanocytes. It can be very aggressive and should be diagnosed and treated early.

More Info

Benign keratosis-like lesions

Benign keratosis-like lesions are non-cancerous skin lesions that may include seborrheic keratoses, which are usually benign warty growths.

More Info

Basal cell carcinoma

Basal cell carcinoma is a common type of skin cancer that usually develops on sun-exposed areas of the skin. It is typically slow-growing and rarely spreads to other parts of the body.

More Info

Actinic keratoses

Actinic keratoses are precancerous skin growths that are often caused by sun exposure. They should be treated to prevent them from developing into skin cancer.

More Info

Vascular lesions

Vascular lesions include various vascular skin conditions such as hemangiomas, which are non-cancerous blood vessel tumors, and port-wine stains.

More Info

Dermatofibroma

Dermatofibromas are benign, hard nodules that often develop on the lower legs. They are typically non-cancerous.
